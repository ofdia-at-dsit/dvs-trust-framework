## 14. The UK CertifID trust mark 

14.a. The UK CertifID trust mark is a unique visual mark that signals that a service has been independently certified against the trust framework and has successfully applied to be listed on the register. The legal provisions related to the trust mark are set out in [section 50 of the Act](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2025/18/section/50).

14.b. The UK CertifID trust mark is a registered trade mark. It is owned by the Secretary of State for Science, Innovation and Technology and OfDIA authorises and licenses its use (on behalf of the Secretary of State) to registered DVS providers as licensed users. 

14.c. Where OfDIA, on behalf of the Secretary of State, issues the licence to use the trust mark to registered DVS providers, it will issue a unique identifying number (‘trust mark identifier’) which links it exclusively to the registered service. If you have multiple registered services, you will be issued with multiple licences to use the trust mark, and a trust mark identifier to be used alongside the trust mark for each service. 

<a id="section-14_1"></a>

### 14.1. Eligibility for licenced use of the trust mark   

14.1.a. You must only use the UK CertifID trust mark with authorisation from OfDIA. Use of the trust mark will be authorised when all the following eligibility conditions are met:

- the service holds a valid certificate against the 1.0 publication of the trust framework ; and

- the service is listed on the register of digital identity and attribute services.

14.1.b. Before OfDIA can authorise your use of the trust mark when the eligibility conditions are met, you must have agreed to the Terms of Use for the trust mark as part of the certification process. Your CAB will make the Terms of Use available to you and will check during the audit process whether you have signed them. 

<a id="section-14_2"></a>

### 14.2. Using and controlling the trust mark

#### 14.2.1. Using the trust mark

14.2.1.a. Use of the trust mark by registered DVS providers is recommended but not mandatory.

14.2.1.b. When you use the trust mark on public-facing materials, such as a website, promotional or marketing materials, you must ensure it and your trust mark identifier are used only in respect of the specific service that has been registered and to which the trust mark identifier relates. For example, you must not use the trust mark in a way that implies that it applies to an organisation as a whole, or to services which have not been registered.

14.2.1.c. You must only display the trust mark on your materials, publications and communications in accordance with the Trust Mark Usage Guidelines (the ‘usage guidelines’). You must also demonstrate to your CAB you have appropriate processes in place for ensuring this. These guidelines, which are set out on GOV.UK, cover rules for trust mark size, positioning, accessibility and contain illustrative examples of correct applications.

14.2.1.d. The usage guidelines may be changed from time to time. Where there are any changes, your CAB will notify you of these changes and you must comply with them within 6 weeks of being notified. You must also demonstrate to your CAB that you have processes in place to ensure your trust mark usage remains compliant with the usage guidelines. 

#### 14.2.2. Controlling the use of the trust mark   

14.2.2.a. You must:

- keep an up-to-date record of all instances where you use the trust mark and your trust mark identifier, and share this information with OfDIA when requested to support its monitoring activities;

- notify OfDIA if you believe the trust mark or your trust mark identifier is being misused, for example by an unauthorised third party; and

- have systems and processes in place to ensure and demonstrate your compliance with this section 14.2.2.

14.2.2.b. You must not: 

- authorise use of the trust mark or your trust mark identifier by others; or

- apply for, obtain, register or use any trade marks, service marks, domain names, company names, trading names or social media accounts that include any part of the trust mark or anything confusingly similar.

#### 14.2.3. Taking corrective actions   

14.2.3.a. You must have processes in place to demonstrate how, upon request from your CAB or OfDIA, you will share information relating to non-conformity and misuse of the trust mark or your trust mark identifier, and how you will take the requested corrective actions within 4 weeks of being notified of the request.

<a id="section-14_3"></a>

### 14.3. Ceasing use of the trust mark

14.3.a. You must have processes in place to ensure your use of the trust mark and your trust mark identifier, including in public-facing materials, will cease within 4 weeks of the occurrence of any of the following (whichever is earlier):

- your service no longer meets the conditions set out in [section 14.1](#section-14_1);

- your usage of the trust mark or your trust mark identifier does not conform to the usage guidelines, and you have not completed corrective actions as requested by your CAB within the prescribed time frame; or

- your receipt of a notification from OfDIA or your CAB that you no longer meet the eligibility criteria and must stop using the trust mark.
